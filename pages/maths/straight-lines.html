1. Brief Recall of Two-Dimensional Geometry

Coordinate System: In a 2D plane, any point is represented by coordinates (x, y) with respect to the x-axis (horizontal) and y-axis (vertical). The point where the x-axis and y-axis intersect is called the origin (0, 0).

Distance Formula: The distance between two points (x1, y1) and (x2, y2) is given by:

d = √((x2 - x1)² + (y2 - y1)²)

Section Formula: The point (x, y) dividing the line segment joining (x1, y1) and (x2, y2) in the ratio m:n is given by:

x = (mx2 + nx1) / (m + n), y = (my2 + ny1) / (m + n)

Midpoint Formula: The midpoint of the line segment joining (x1, y1) and (x2, y2) is:

((x1 + x2) / 2, (y1 + y2) / 2)

2. Slope of a Line

Definition: The slope (or gradient) of a line measures its steepness. If a line passes through two points (x1, y1) and (x2, y2), its slope m is given by:

m = (y2 - y1) / (x2 - x1)

If (x1 = x2), the slope is undefined, indicating a vertical line.

Special Cases:

  • Horizontal line: Slope m = 0.
  • Vertical line: Slope is undefined.

Slope of Line with Respect to Axes: If a line makes an angle θ with the positive x-axis (measured counterclockwise), then:

m = tan(θ)

3. Angle Between Two Lines

If two lines have slopes m1 and m2, the angle θ between them is given by:

tan(θ) = |(m2 - m1) / (1 + m1m2)|

Special Cases:

  • Parallel lines: m1 = m2 (angle = 0°).
  • Perpendicular lines: m1m2 = -1 (angle = 90°).

4. Various Forms of the Equation of a Line

Equation of a Line Parallel to Axes:

  • Parallel to x-axis: y = c
  • Parallel to y-axis: x = k

Point-Slope Form: If a line has a slope m and passes through a point (x1, y1), its equation is:

y - y1 = m(x - x1)

Slope-Intercept Form: If a line has a slope m and y-intercept c, its equation is:

y = mx + c

Two-Point Form: If a line passes through two points (x1, y1) and (x2, y2), its equation is:

y - y1 = ((y2 - y1) / (x2 - x1))(x - x1)

Intercept Form: If a line cuts the x-axis at a and the y-axis at b, its equation is:

(x/a) + (y/b) = 1

General Form: The most general form of a line is:

ax + by + c = 0

Here, a, b, and c are constants. The slope of this line is -a/b.

5. Distance of a Point from a Line

To find the perpendicular distance of a point (x1, y1) from a line given by the equation ax + by + c = 0:

d = |(ax1 + by1 + c)| / √(a² + b²)

6. Additional Concepts

Collinearity of Three Points: Three points (x1, y1), (x2, y2), and (x3, y3) are collinear if the area of the triangle formed by them is zero, i.e.:

(1/2) * |x1(y2 - y3) + x2(y3 - y1) + x3(y1 - y2)| = 0

Intersection of Two Lines: To find the point of intersection of two lines given by:

a1x + b1y + c1 = 0 and a2x + b2y + c2 = 0

solve the system of equations simultaneously.

Family of Lines: The equation of a family of lines passing through the point of intersection of two given lines L1 = 0 and L2 = 0 is:

L = L1 + λL2 = 0

where λ is a parameter.

Bisectors of Angles Between Two Lines: The equation of the angle bisectors of two lines a1x + b1y + c1 = 0 and a2x + b2y + c2 = 0 is given by:

(a1x + b1y + c1) / (a1² + b1²) = ±(a2x + b2y + c2) / (a2² + b2²)

Summary

A straight line can be represented in multiple forms depending on given information (e.g., slope, intercepts, points). The concept of slope is fundamental in understanding the orientation of a line. Various forms like point-slope, slope-intercept, two-point, and general form allow flexibility in solving geometrical problems. Knowing how to calculate distances, angles, and intersection points between lines is crucial for applications in coordinate geometry.
